## Project Summary

I designed a dedicated Business Portal for Coins.ph partners to help them manage business transactions more securely and efficiently. The goal was to move partners away from using the same experience built for regular wallet users and create a product that better matched business needs such as wallet visibility, transaction tracking, approvals, team access, and operational control.

## The Problem

Coins.ph already provided technical and financial solutions for business clients through APIs and services such as sell orders, cash-in settlement, and exchange. However, the partner-facing experience was not designed specifically for business use.

Business clients were using the standard wallet portal, which created several product and UX gaps:

- The experience was built for individual users, not organizations
- Access to features was too broad for business operations
- Security and control were not strong enough for team-based usage
- Transaction monitoring and reporting were not optimized for operational workflows
- The product did not communicate the level of trust, structure, and clarity expected from a business tool

This created friction for existing partners and limited the product's ability to feel like a true business platform.

![Before — the existing consumer-oriented portal experience](/projects/business/business-before.webp)

## Product Goal

The objective was to design a more secure and business-ready portal that could support partner operations while strengthening trust in the platform.

The product needed to:

- Create a clearer separation between consumer and business experiences
- Improve visibility into balances, transactions, and wallet activity
- Support business workflows such as approvals, team access, and reporting
- Make the platform feel more credible and reliable for organizations
- Help retain current partners while making the product more attractive to future ones

## My Role

I led the product design and UI/UX direction for the Business Portal experience. My work included:

- Translating business goals into product flows
- Identifying key pain points in the existing experience
- Defining the information architecture for business users
- Creating wireframes and interface explorations
- Iterating with stakeholders and internal teams
- Designing high-fidelity screens for the final portal experience

## Users and Needs

The primary users were business partners and internal stakeholders who needed a portal that could support day-to-day financial operations with more clarity and control.

Their needs included:

- Quick access to wallet balances and account information
- Easier monitoring of transactions across cash-in, cash-out, and transfers
- Clearer transaction states and history
- Safer access for business teams
- A more structured, professional experience aligned with business expectations

## Key Design Challenges

This project was not just a visual redesign. It required rethinking the product through a business-use lens.

Some of the main challenges were:

- Adapting a consumer-oriented platform for organizational use
- Simplifying complex financial actions without removing critical information
- Designing for trust and control in a product handling sensitive transactions
- Making the interface feel lightweight and usable while supporting more robust business functionality
- Balancing stakeholder requirements with the practical needs of partners

![Additional design explorations](/projects/business/business-others-01.webp)

## Design Approach

I approached the project by first understanding the current product experience and identifying where it failed to support business workflows.

From there, I focused on three areas:

**1. Business-first structure**
I reorganized the experience around the tasks business users needed most, such as managing wallets, reviewing transactions, cashing in, cashing out, handling approvals, and managing users.

**2. Clarity and visibility**
Because this product deals with financial activity, the interface needed to make balances, statuses, transaction records, and related actions easier to scan and understand.

**3. Trust and operational confidence**
The design had to feel more secure, controlled, and credible than the standard consumer portal. This influenced both the interface structure and the overall visual tone of the product.

## From Discovery to Solution

The team aligned on business goals and identified likely risks and failure points early in the process. We also reviewed comparable products and existing experiences to understand how similar financial workflows were being handled.

I then translated these insights into wireframes to explore the product structure and user flow. These early concepts helped validate navigation, layout, and core task flows before moving into polished designs.

![Wireframes exploring product structure and user flows](/projects/business/business-wireframes.webp)

The design evolved through multiple rounds of feedback with stakeholders, allowing us to refine both the business requirements and the user experience at the same time. Once the core structure was validated, I developed high-fidelity mockups and supported the experience through early partner-facing iterations.

## Final Solution

The final Business Portal introduced a more structured and business-ready experience centered around operational tasks.

Key improvements included:

- A dedicated navigation tailored to business actions
- Clearer wallet management for both PHP and crypto balances
- Transaction history views with filters and status visibility
- Cash-in and cash-out flows designed around payment methods and operational review
- Support for approvals and team-based usage
- A cleaner, more professional UI that better reflects a business platform
