## Project Summary

I led the redesign of the Coins.ph mobile app experience, with a strong focus on improving the cryptocurrency wallet journey. The app had remained visually and structurally similar for years, while the product itself had evolved significantly. As more cryptocurrencies and related features were introduced, the experience became harder to navigate and less aligned with how crypto users actually interacted with the product.

The redesign aimed to modernize the app, improve usability across key crypto flows, and create a more focused experience for users managing multiple digital assets within the platform.

## The Problem

The Coins.ph mobile app had served users for many years, but its interface and interaction model had not evolved at the same pace as the product.

Several issues had become increasingly visible:

- The visual design was beginning to feel dated
- The Android and iOS experiences had inconsistencies
- The growing number of supported cryptocurrencies made navigation more difficult
- Crypto-related actions were mixed into a broader wallet experience that was not optimized for crypto use cases
- Some modules and services shown within the experience were not especially relevant to users managing crypto assets

As the product expanded, the app needed more than a visual refresh. It needed a clearer structure that better reflected current user behavior and product priorities.

![Brainstorming session](/projects/coins-mobile/brainstorming.webp)

## Product Goal

The goal of the redesign was to create a more modern and usable mobile experience, especially for crypto users who needed faster access to their assets, prices, balances, and actions.

The redesign focused on:

- Modernizing the overall UI without losing product familiarity
- Making crypto assets easier to browse and manage
- Improving navigation across a growing set of currencies and features
- Creating a more focused wallet experience for crypto-related tasks
- Aligning the product experience more consistently across platforms
- Establishing a stronger design foundation for future mobile work

## My Role

I led the product design and UI/UX work for the redesign. My responsibilities included:

- Framing the redesign problem
- Initiating and structuring the design sprint process
- Translating product and business needs into flows and interface concepts
- Producing wireframes and prototypes
- Collaborating with product, engineering, and internal stakeholders
- Conducting and synthesizing user testing inputs
- Refining the final high-fidelity designs
- Preparing UI documentation and handoff materials for implementation

## Why the Redesign Was Needed

The redesign was driven by both product growth and experience debt.

Coins.ph had evolved beyond the structure of its earlier mobile experience. As more crypto assets and related user actions were introduced, the interface became less efficient for the very users it needed to support. Navigation, content priority, and feature grouping no longer felt as intentional as the product deserved.

The project was therefore not just about making the app look newer. It was about rethinking how the experience should support users in a more mature crypto product ecosystem.

![Before — the existing mobile app experience](/projects/coins-mobile/coins-before.webp)

## Discovery and Design Sprint

Because this was one of my first major projects at Coins, I wanted to avoid jumping straight into screens. I initiated a customized Design Sprint process tailored to the team's workflow and decision-making environment.

This helped create alignment early around:

- The core problem we were solving
- The most important user needs
- Risks that could affect the redesign
- Nice-to-have ideas versus high-priority improvements
- The opportunity areas within the crypto wallet experience

The sprint gave the team a shared framework for evaluating the redesign and helped move discussions beyond visual preference toward product reasoning.

![Wireframes exploring product structure and user flows](/projects/coins-mobile/wireframes.webp)

## Research and Testing

After the sprint, I translated the initial insights into low-fidelity concepts and wireframes. These were used to explore structure, hierarchy, and possible navigation patterns before committing to polished UI.

To validate the direction, I worked with a Product Analyst to conduct user testing and interviews using a low-fidelity prototype. Because the redesign was still confidential at the time, participants were selected internally from employees who actively used Coins.ph. The group included users with different wallet behaviors and levels of familiarity with the product, from crypto-focused activity to more general payment-related use cases.

This phase helped us understand:

- Which parts of the experience felt familiar versus confusing
- How users expected crypto assets and actions to be organized
- What information they needed surfaced earlier
- Where the navigation and grouping still needed refinement

After iterating on the low-fidelity direction, we moved into high-fidelity mockups and conducted another round of testing and interviews with a larger participant set. These sessions helped refine the final visual hierarchy, interaction clarity, and screen-level decisions.

## Design Direction

The redesign centered on making the crypto experience feel more intentional, more legible, and more product-focused.

I guided the design in three main directions:

**1. A dedicated crypto-focused experience**
Instead of treating crypto as just one part of a broader wallet screen, the redesign gave it a clearer and more purposeful home. This made it easier for users to view holdings, understand distribution, and access relevant actions.

**2. Better hierarchy and usability**
Balances, asset lists, prices, and transactional actions needed to be easier to scan and act on. The redesign improved content hierarchy so users could move through the product with less friction.

**3. Modernization without disruption**
Because this was an established product, the redesign had to improve the experience without making it feel unfamiliar. The goal was to modernize the UI while preserving enough continuity for existing users.

## Final Solution

The final design introduced a cleaner and more structured mobile experience centered around crypto use cases.

Key improvements included:

- A dedicated cryptocurrency wallet screen
- Better visibility into asset distribution and total crypto balance
- Easier access to high-frequency actions such as send, receive, price checking, and conversion
- A clearer asset list for managing multiple currencies
- More focused detail screens for individual assets
- A more modern visual language across the mobile app
- Improved consistency in UI patterns and components

The redesigned experience helped shift the app from a legacy interface toward a more focused product experience that better matched the platform's evolving crypto offering.

## Design System and Handoff

One of the broader opportunities I identified during the project was the need for stronger design documentation.

At the time, some projects lacked the level of documentation needed for continuity and efficient collaboration. As part of this redesign, I made sure the new mobile wallet UI was supported by more complete documentation, including visual references, UI patterns, and handoff assets.

This served two purposes:

- It made implementation smoother for developers
- It created a more useful design reference for future product and design work

Rather than treating handoff as the final step, I approached it as part of building a more scalable product design practice.

## Outcome

This project helped reposition the Coins.ph mobile app as a more modern and product-aware experience, particularly for crypto users.

The redesign created:

- A stronger experience for navigating multiple crypto assets
- A more focused structure for high-frequency wallet actions
- Better alignment between the product's maturity and its interface
- A more consistent foundation for future mobile improvements

It also reinforced the importance of treating redesigns not as visual refreshes, but as opportunities to realign the product with evolving user behavior.

## Key Learnings

This project taught me several things that continue to shape how I work as a product designer.

**Design quality improves when structure comes before screens**
Starting with a tailored sprint process created stronger alignment and helped the team discuss the product more clearly before moving into UI.

**Iteration is strongest when testing happens early and again later**
Using both low-fidelity and high-fidelity testing gave us feedback at the right moments. Early testing helped shape the structure; later testing helped refine the experience.

**User segmentation matters more than it first appears**
One of the important takeaways from research was that usage patterns within the same product can differ significantly. A redesign needs to account not only for general users, but also for higher-frequency and more specialized users whose needs may shape the product more heavily.

**Handoff is part of product design, not just delivery**
Clear documentation and reusable UI references are not side tasks. They are part of creating a product that can evolve more effectively over time.
