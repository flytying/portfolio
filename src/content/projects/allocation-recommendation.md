## Project Summary

Allocation Recommendation is a product exploration focused on improving how leads are allocated to publisher partners. The goal was to support a more intelligent allocation process using signals such as speed score, publisher rating, and priority, rather than relying only on manual judgment or static rules.

This project also reflects a newer way of working in my design practice: combining discovery with AI-assisted prototyping to turn product ideas into reviewable concepts much earlier. Instead of waiting for a longer static design cycle, I used rapid prototyping to help stakeholders react to a tangible solution sooner.

## The Problem

Lead allocation is a high-impact operational problem. When leads are not routed effectively, the business risks slower response times, weaker partner performance, and less efficient distribution overall.

The challenge was not simply to automate lead assignment. It was to explore how a product experience could make allocation logic more understandable, more actionable, and better aligned with business priorities. The solution needed to account for multiple signals, including publisher performance and operational urgency, while still being clear enough for stakeholders to evaluate and trust.

## My Role

I led the product design exploration for this initiative, starting from discovery and moving into AI-assisted prototyping. My responsibilities included:

- Working with stakeholders to define the problem
- Identifying the key signals that influenced allocation decisions
- Translating early business thinking into a testable product direction
- Creating rapid prototypes in Figma Make
- Iterating on the concept based on stakeholder feedback

## Discovery

The project began with stakeholder conversations to better understand what problem we were actually trying to solve. These discussions helped clarify:

- What inputs mattered most in allocation decisions
- What the business wanted the system to optimize for
- Where the current process created friction or inefficiency
- What tradeoffs and constraints needed to be considered

This phase was important because it grounded the work in a real operational problem rather than treating AI as a feature in search of a use case.

## Prototype 1: Early Concept for Stakeholder Reaction

Once the problem and opportunity were clearer, I created an initial prototype using Figma Make. The goal of this first version was speed. Rather than trying to perfect the interface too early, I focused on turning the concept into something visible and discussable.

This prototype was used to:

- Make the idea tangible for stakeholders
- Gather early reactions to the allocation concept
- Uncover open questions in the workflow
- Pressure-test whether the proposed direction made sense

The value of this stage was not polish. It was creating enough substance for meaningful product discussion to happen earlier.

## Prototype 2: Refined Direction Based on Feedback

After the first round of stakeholder review, I created a second prototype informed by the feedback gathered from the initial concept. This version was more refined in both flow and interaction model, and gave the team a clearer view of how the experience could work in practice.

The second prototype helped:

- Clarify the proposed product direction
- Refine the flow based on stakeholder concerns and questions
- Move the conversation from abstract discussion toward a more aligned solution
- Provide a stronger reference point for future prioritization

Together, these two iterations reflect my current workflow: using AI-assisted rapid prototyping to shorten the gap between discovery and solution evaluation.

## Outcome So Far

Although the project has not yet moved into active development, the work delivered value by:

- Clarifying the allocation opportunity
- Helping stakeholders evaluate the idea earlier
- Surfacing questions before deeper investment
- Creating a more concrete direction for future product planning

## Current Status

The project is currently in backlog. The prototype work served as an early exploration and alignment tool, giving the team a stronger foundation if and when the initiative moves forward.
