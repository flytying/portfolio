## Project Summary

Publisher Quoting is a workflow redesign initiative focused on improving how the operations team manages communication, reviews campaign requirements, and consolidates approved quotes for sales. The goal was to move this process away from fragmented tools and manual handoffs into a more structured platform experience.

This project also reflects my AI-assisted workflow. After discovery and stakeholder alignment, I used rapid prototyping to turn early ideas into reviewable concepts quickly. Across the project, I created three prototypes that helped shape the product direction before arriving at the final mockup now being used as the basis for development.

## The Problem

The existing quoting workflow was fragmented across multiple tools and handoffs. Communication between ops and publisher partners happened mostly through email, while campaign requirements had to be reviewed separately in Monday.com.

This created several workflow issues. Ops had to move between email threads and a long Monday.com form just to gather the information needed to do their job. The form also contained details that were not always relevant to the ops task, making it harder to quickly identify the campaign requirements that actually mattered for quoting.

Another problem appeared later in the process. Once quotes were approved, ops still had to rely on email to consolidate and send them to sales. This added another manual step, increased the risk of delays or missing context, and made the workflow harder to manage from end to end.

The opportunity was not simply to replace email with a platform. It was to create a clearer operational experience that could centralize communication, surface only the most relevant campaign requirements, and support quote consolidation before sending approved quotes to sales.

## Product Goal

The goal was to design a platform that could support the quoting workflow more clearly from end to end by:

- Centralizing communication between ops and publishers
- Giving ops a cleaner view of campaign requirements
- Reducing reliance on long email threads and cluttered forms
- Consolidating approved quotes in one place before sending them to sales
- Making the process easier to track, manage, and scale

## My Role

I led the product design work for this initiative from discovery through final mockup. My responsibilities included:

- Working with stakeholders to define the workflow problem
- Understanding how the current process worked across teams
- Identifying opportunities to reduce manual friction
- Creating and iterating on prototypes to explore possible solutions
- Refining the product direction through stakeholder feedback
- Producing the final mockup used for development

## Discovery

The project started with discovery conversations and stakeholder interviews to understand how the quoting workflow currently operated, where friction existed, and what the team needed from a more structured solution.

This phase helped clarify:

- How communication moved between ops, publishers, and sales
- Where delays and inefficiencies were happening
- What information ops actually needed to see
- Which parts of the workflow created unnecessary clutter
- What the platform would need to support operationally

This ensured the work stayed grounded in real workflow pain points rather than becoming a surface-level tool replacement.

## Final Solution

The final design reimagined the quoting process as a structured platform experience rather than a workflow split across email and Monday.com.

Instead of forcing ops to piece together context from different tools, the platform was designed to:

- Centralize communication with publisher partners
- Present campaign requirements in a clearer, less cluttered way
- Highlight the information most relevant to the ops task
- Support quote consolidation inside the platform
- Create a cleaner handoff of approved quotes to sales

The result was a more organized operational flow that reduced manual overhead and made the quoting process easier to manage from start to finish.

## Outcome So Far

This project delivered value by helping the team move from a fragmented operational process toward a more intentional productized workflow. The rapid prototyping approach made it possible to explore, review, and refine the solution much earlier, creating stronger alignment before development.

## Current Status

The project is currently in development. The prototype iterations and final mockup helped validate the workflow direction, align stakeholders, and create a clearer foundation for implementation.
